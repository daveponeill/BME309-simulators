How to read this

Z-plane: The unit circle separates stable (inside) from unstable (outside) pole locations. The transfer function magnitude at any frequency Ω is the ratio of distances from that point on the unit circle to all zeros (numerator) divided by distances to all poles (denominator).

Gain near a pole: If a pole is at radius r, its distance to the unit circle ≈ (1 − r), giving a local peak gain of approximately 1/(1 − r). A pole at r = 0.9 yields ~10× gain; r = 0.99 yields ~100×.
